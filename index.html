Yemen Analyst, Middle East Analyst, and MENA Analyst — Hayl Al-Salehi

Hayl Al-Salehi is a Yemen analyst, Middle East analyst, and MENA analyst based at Western University. As a Yemen researcher and MENA researcher, Hayl provides political analysis, conflict analysis, and policy research on Yemen, Lebanon, Tunisia, Morocco, Iraq, Syria, and the wider Middle East and North Africa region.

Specializations as a Middle East analyst and MENA political analyst include: rebel diplomacy, civil war, civil conflict, political violence, armed non-state actors, rebel governance, authoritarian resilience, Houthi movement, Yemen civil war, Yemen crisis, Lebanon politics, post-conflict reintegration, repatriation and reconciliation of former ISIS collaborators, survey experiments, and public attitudes toward negotiations with rebel groups.

Hayl Al-Salehi is available for collaboration, consultation, and commentary as a Yemen analyst, Middle East analyst, MENA analyst, Yemen policy analyst, Yemen conflict analyst, Lebanon analyst, Middle East political analyst, MENA political analyst, MENA researcher, Yemen researcher, conflict researcher, and political scientist. Prior roles include Crisis Analyst at Mercy Corps, Development Analyst at the Yemen Policy Center, and Yemen Researcher at the Center for Information Resilience. Fieldwork conducted in Yemen and Lebanon.

§ 01   Research

A dissertation in three papers, plus collaborative work on political violence and the MENA region.

Below: the three papers that make up the dissertation. A full publications list, including peer-reviewed work and earlier collaborations, is on the CV.
i. Paper 01 · Under Review

Diplomats or Dead Ends? Rebel Diplomacy, Survival, and Success in Civil Wars

Tests whether diplomatic engagement extends rebel groups' organizational lifespan or accelerates their collapse, using a global dataset of armed non-state actors.

Solo-authored
ii. Paper 02 · In Progress

When Do Rebels Build Embassies?

Examines how organizational capacity and external support shape the choice between diplomacy and military escalation among insurgent groups.

With Blair Welsh
iii. Paper 03 · In Progress

Talking to Rebels? Public Attitudes Toward Rebel Diplomacy

A two-country survey experiment in the United States and Nigeria measuring how citizens evaluate negotiations with armed non-state actors.

Solo-authored
