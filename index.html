About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at the University of Western Ontario, where I study rebel diplomacy, civil conflict, and state–non-state actor relations. My dissertation, Rebel Diplomacy: Determinants, Consequences, and Public Attitudes, examines when and why armed non-state actors pursue diplomatic recognition, the consequences of those choices for conflict outcomes, and how the public in different countries perceives negotiations with rebel groups.

My broader research spans political violence, authoritarian resilience in the Middle East and North Africa, and the reintegration of former combatants. I have conducted fieldwork in Yemen and Lebanon, and have worked as a crisis analyst and policy researcher with organizations including Mercy Corps, the Yemen Policy Center, and the Center for Information Resilience.

Before coming to Western, I completed an M.A. in International Affairs with Distinction at the Lebanese American University (2021) and a B.A. in Psychology at the American University of Beirut (2019).
